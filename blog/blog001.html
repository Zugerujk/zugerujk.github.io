My nephew says "bruh"

I was just playing Minecraft with my nephew, who's a little over 5 years old now. I had a good time, although idk if he did; he's a little self-defeatist, which is depressing for a 5 year old to be. He thanked me for playing with him, 'cuz he's a good kid. He's such a good kid, I wish the absolute best for him. But you know what really weirds me out?

He says "bruh". "C'mon bruh", to be specific. He's developmentally behind (can't read, can't spell), but yet speaks wholly unlike a 5 year old should. I feel soooooo old when he says "bruh". It's creepy. It's terrifying. He sounds like a South Park baby, saying "c'mon bruh". I just watched Season 18 (the one with Pewdiepie), so that's instantly what my mind jumps to. He watches a bunch of Minecraft YouTubers unsupervised, which definitely isn't good, but damn. He didn't even know what a "word" WAS, but he's saying "bruh". Serious bruh moment.

I saw Past Lives on July 4th, at the AMC Theatres at Quail Springs Mall. 'Twas about an hour drive from where I live. It was a really phenomenal movie - extremely good, and accomplishes everything it wants to do perfectly. I have a review for it on my Letterboxd. Anyways, it takes place in 2024, and I wonder. Will my nephew be able to read in 2024? What new, hip lingo will he be saying in 2024? It's terrifying to think about.
